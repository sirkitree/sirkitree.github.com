      <div class="qw-card">
        <h2 class="qw-title" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">Historical Context: "The Lightbenders"</h2>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p>
            <strong>Spectra was likely discovered mid-timeline</strong> (discipline 5 of 9), after immediate survival needs were addressed but before exotic applications like gravity manipulation were explored. Its discovery probably came from observing thread interactions with sunlight—carriers noticing their threads could bend rays, create colors, or render objects translucent.
          </p>

          <h3 class="qw-subtitle">Discovery Pattern</h3>
          <p>
            Unlike Infomancy (systematically developed for observation) or Corpus (urgently needed for healing), Spectra was likely discovered through play or accident:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Thread carrier experimenting with light filtering</li>
            <li>Noticing threads could "catch" and redirect sunlight</li>
            <li>Children playing with rainbow-making threads</li>
            <li>Camouflage techniques emerging from necessity (hiding from predators/threats)</li>
          </ul>

          <h3 class="qw-subtitle">Cultural Significance</h3>
          <p>
            In Weaver culture, Spectra practitioners are called <strong>"Lightbenders"</strong> (neutral), <strong>"Illusionists"</strong> (artistic), or <strong>"Deceivers"</strong> (pejorative when used for manipulation).
          </p>
          <p><strong>Traditional sayings:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li><em>"Light reveals truth; light conceals it."</em></li>
            <li><em>"The eye sees what the mind believes."</em></li>
            <li><em>"All illusion requires one truth: that you are willing to be deceived."</em></li>
          </ul>

          <h3 class="qw-subtitle">Modern Practice & Reputation</h3>
          <p>
            Spectra has <strong>complex social standing</strong>:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Valued:</strong> Scouts, spies, entertainers, artists, architects (light design)</li>
            <li><strong>Distrusted:</strong> Association with deception, trickery, false appearances</li>
            <li><strong>Regulated:</strong> Many societies require disclosure when illusions are used in commercial/legal contexts</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            <strong>Shadow Syndicate</strong> employs master Spectra practitioners for espionage and infiltration. <strong>Forge Masters</strong> use Spectra for aesthetic enhancements (color-changing metals, luminescent crafts). <strong>Quantum Scholars</strong> study Spectra's quantum optical basis extensively.
          </p>
          <p>
            <strong>The Illusion Paradox:</strong> Spectra practitioners must simultaneously:
          </p>
          <ol>
            <li>Create convincing illusions (requiring deep immersion in imagined reality)</li>
            <li>Maintain awareness of actual reality (to avoid perceptual disorders)</li>
          </ol>
          <p>
            This "reality anchoring" challenge means extended Spectra use can cause dissociative symptoms—practitioners who "lose themselves" in their own illusions.
          </p>
        </div>

Threads create plasmonic cavities that trap and redirect photons, enabling control of light propagation at the nanoscale. Invisibility works by routing photons around objects through plasmonic waveguides.
          </p>

          </p>

    <section id="novice-spells" style="max-width: 900px; margin: 4rem 0;">
      <h2 class="qw-title" style="margin-bottom: 2rem; color: var(--qw-spectra);">Foundational Spells (Novice Tier)</h2>

      <div id="illuminate" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">1. Illuminate (Light-Craft)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates visible light source from thin air—a glowing orb, beam, or diffuse illumination. Brightness adjustable from candle-level to sunlight intensity. Can be colored or white light. Does not produce heat.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Temporary (sustained concentration, or set for fixed duration—1 minute to 1 hour)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Very Low to Moderate (3-5% for candle-level sustained 1 hour, 10-15% for sunbright sustained 10 minutes)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Practical illumination (exploring dark areas, reading, working at night)</li>
            <li>Signaling (light beacons, communication over distance)</li>
            <li>Entertainment (light shows, mood lighting)</li>
            <li>Combat (sudden bright flash to disorient, sustained light to remove enemy's darkness advantage)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="veil" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">2. Veil (Blur-Shroud)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Makes subject harder to see by bending light around edges, creating blurry outline or translucent appearance. Not true invisibility (more advanced), but effective camouflage in low light or at distance.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Temporary (sustained concentration, typically 5-30 minutes before thread fatigue)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Low-Moderate (8-15% reserves for person-sized veil sustained 15 minutes)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Stealth (avoiding detection in shadows, fog, dim light)</li>
            <li>Hunting (getting closer to prey)</li>
            <li>Escape (fleeing by becoming harder to see)</li>
            <li>Privacy (blurring windows, creating visual barriers)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="mirage" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">3. Mirage (Static-Illusion)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates simple, stationary visual illusion—an image that appears in space but has no physical substance. Can be object, person, text, or abstract pattern. Image is flat (2D) or simple 3D. Does not move.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Temporary (sustained 5 minutes to 1 hour with concentration, or "set and forget" for simpler images)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Low-Moderate (5-10% for simple image like text or basic shape, 15-25% for complex detailed image like realistic person)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Signage (creating text, symbols, directions in mid-air)</li>
            <li>Deception (fake obstacles, false walls, decoy objects)</li>
            <li>Art (visual installations, light sculptures)</li>
            <li>Communication (visual messages, diagrams)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="prism" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">4. Prism (Spectrum-Split)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Bends and splits light into component colors, creating rainbow effects, refracting beams, or separating white light into spectrum. Can magnify, focus, or disperse light. Essentially creates invisible lenses and prisms in air.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Temporary (sustained concentration or brief effect)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Low (5-10% reserves for typical effects)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Creating rainbows (aesthetic, signaling)</li>
            <li>Concentrating sunlight (fire-starting, solar heating)</li>
            <li>Magnification (makeshift telescope/microscope)</li>
            <li>Light weapons (focused beam for cutting/burning)</li>
            <li>Scientific analysis (splitting light to analyze composition)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="flash" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">5. Flash (Photon-Burst)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates sudden, intense burst of light to temporarily blind, disorient, or signal. Brightness ranges from camera flash to lightning-level brilliance. Brief duration (fraction of second to few seconds).</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Instantaneous (flash lasts <1 second) to brief (sustained bright light for 2-5 seconds)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate (10-15% for blinding flash, 20-25% for sustained beam)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Combat (blinding enemies, creating distraction)</li>
            <li>Escape (covering retreat with flash)</li>
            <li>Signaling (emergency beacon, flare substitute)</li>
            <li>Distress signal (calling for help)</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Ethical/Legal Status:</strong> Flash is combat magic. Many societies regulate it with prohibited intensity levels and require combat training certification.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="darksight" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">6. Darksight (Spectrum-Shift)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Enables practitioner to see in darkness by shifting their vision into infrared spectrum (detecting heat) or amplifying available light. Can also shift to ultraviolet (seeing fluorescent materials, bodily fluids) or other normally invisible wavelengths.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Temporary (sustained concentration, 10 minutes to several hours)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate (12-18% reserves for 1-2 hours of enhanced vision)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Navigation in darkness (caves, night travel, blackout buildings)</li>
            <li>Tracking (following heat signatures, seeing recent warm handprints)</li>
            <li>Security (detecting hidden people via body heat)</li>
            <li>Forensics (UV reveals bodily fluids, trace evidence)</li>
            <li>Combat (seeing in smoke, darkness)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="phantom" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">7. Phantom (Moving-Illusion)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates simple moving holographic image—a person walking, object moving, animal running. More advanced than Mirage (static image) because motion must be convincingly animated. Limited complexity—simple motions only at novice level.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Temporary (sustained concentration for duration of movement, typically 30 seconds to 5 minutes)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate-High (20-30% for simple moving figure over 2-3 minutes)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Deception (creating false patrol routes, phantom intruders)</li>
            <li>Distraction (drawing attention away from real activity)</li>
            <li>Entertainment (illusion shows, storytelling)</li>
            <li>Training (creating moving targets, practice opponents)</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section id="intermediate-spells" style="max-width: 900px; margin: 4rem 0;">
      <h2 class="qw-title" style="margin-bottom: 2rem; color: var(--qw-spectra);">Intermediate Spells (Practitioner Tier)</h2>

      <div id="vanish" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">8. Vanish (True-Invisibility)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Renders subject completely invisible by routing light perfectly around them—true optical cloaking. Unlike Veil (blur/translucency), Vanish achieves near-perfect invisibility. Requires intense concentration and thread expenditure.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Temporary (sustained concentration, 1-10 minutes maximum before thread exhaustion or mental fatigue)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Very High (35-50% reserves for person-sized invisibility sustained 5-10 minutes; exhausting)</p>
          <p><strong>Ethical/Legal Status:</strong> Vanish is <strong>heavily regulated or outright banned</strong> in many societies. Considered enabling crime (theft, assault, espionage). Military/intelligence use with authorization.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="project" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">9. Project (Hologram)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates three-dimensional light constructs—realistic holographic images that can move, change, and interact visually with environment. Can project people, objects, creatures, or abstract forms. Pure illusion—no substance.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Sustained (minutes to hours depending on complexity)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate-High (25-45% depending on size, detail, animation complexity)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong> Communication (projecting distant person), entertainment, tactical deception, education, artistic expression</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="blind" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">10. Blind (Light-Overload)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Floods target's vision with overwhelming light, causing temporary blindness. Can affect single person or area. Blindness lasts minutes to hours depending on intensity.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Flash is instantaneous, blindness persists 5-30 minutes</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Low-Moderate (15-25%)</p>
          <p><strong>Ethical/Legal:</strong> Classified as weapon in many jurisdictions, banned for civilian use in some regions</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="spectro-scan" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">11. Spectro-Scan (Material-Analysis)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Separates light into component wavelengths, revealing hidden information. Can analyze material composition by spectral signature, detect invisible inks/marks, identify light sources, or reveal spectroscopic data for scientific analysis.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Active analysis (sustained minutes)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate (20-30%)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong> Material identification, detecting forgeries, finding hidden messages, art authentication, chemical analysis, astronomical observation</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="mirror-image" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">12. Mirror-Image (Duplicate-Self)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates 1-4 illusory duplicates of caster that move and act convincingly. Duplicates mimic movements, confusing attackers about which is real. Pure visual illusion—no substance.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Sustained (5-15 minutes)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate-High (25-40%, scales with number of duplicates)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong> Combat (confusing attackers), escape, performance, tactical deception</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="light-bend" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">13. Light-Bend (Refraction-Control)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Manipulates light path by controlling refraction—can create mirages, make objects appear displaced, magnify/diminish, or create optical distortions. Advanced version can simulate impossible optics.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Sustained (minutes to hours)</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate-High (25-40%)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong> Creating illusions, magnification, periscope effects (seeing around corners), hiding objects, artistic displays</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="dazzle" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">14. Dazzle (Distraction-Pattern)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates mesmerizing, hypnotic light patterns that capture attention and impair cognitive function. Targets become distracted, suggestible, or disoriented. Can affect multiple observers.</p>
          <p><strong>Duration:</strong> Active pattern (1-10 minutes), effects linger briefly after</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate (20-35%)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong> Crowd control, interrogation (ethical concerns), stage performance, therapeutic uses (meditation aid), tactical distraction</p>
          <p><strong>Ethical/Legal:</strong> Mind-affecting technique raises consent issues, banned in some jurisdictions, regulated as psychological weapon</p>
        </div>
      </div>

    <section id="advanced-spells" style="max-width: 900px; margin: 4rem 0;">
      <h2 class="qw-title" style="margin-bottom: 2rem; color: var(--qw-spectra);">Advanced Spells (Master Tier)</h2>

      <div id="true-invisibility" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">15. True Invisibility (Quantum-Cloak)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Achieves genuine invisibility by placing object in quantum superposition of "visible" and "invisible" states, biasing toward invisible. Unlike simple light-bending (which leaves shimmer), Quantum-Cloak makes target genuinely undetectable to EM spectrum observation.</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Extreme (70-90%, scales with object size)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Perfect stealth (no shimmer/distortion)</li>
            <li>Scientific observation without interference</li>
            <li>Espionage</li>
            <li>Tactical infiltration</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Limitation:</strong> Subject cannot emit light (total darkness experience), extremely thread-expensive, collapse to visible if observed strongly enough (quantum measurement), movement risks decoherence.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="solid-light" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">16. Solid Light (Photon-Matter)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates tangible constructs from coherent photons—hard-light bridges, walls, weapons, or tools. Unlike holograms (intangible illusions), Solid Light has physical mass and structural integrity. Treats light as building material.</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Very High (60-80%, proportional to structure size/strength)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Emergency bridges</li>
            <li>Instant barriers</li>
            <li>Light-weapons (swords, spears)</li>
            <li>Architectural supports</li>
            <li>Tactical constructs</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Limitation:</strong> Requires constant thread maintenance (structures collapse when threads depleted), vulnerable to EM interference, cannot create complex mechanisms (no moving parts), thread-expensive.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="probability-vision" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">17. Probability Vision (Quantum-Sight)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Enhances vision to perceive quantum probability clouds—seeing all possible positions an object might occupy simultaneously. Allows predicting movement (seeing probability distribution of future positions), detecting quantum uncertainty, or identifying illusions (which lack quantum probability structure).</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate-High (40-60% sustained)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Combat prediction (seeing enemy's probable movements)</li>
            <li>Illusion detection (fakes have no probability structure)</li>
            <li>Quantum research</li>
            <li>Tactical advantage</li>
            <li>Scientific observation</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Integration with Divination:</strong> Probability Vision provides visual component for Probabilistic Divination—seeing temporal probability as literal ghostly images overlaying present.</p>
          <p><strong>Limitation:</strong> Overwhelming sensory input (seeing hundreds of probability branches), psychological disorientation, works only on quantum-scale uncertainty (not true future-sight), mental fatigue severe.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="chromatic-manipulation" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">18. Chromatic Manipulation (Emotion-Color)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Manipulates emotional states through precise color/wavelength exposure—deeper than Luxomancy's general emotional influence. Can induce specific states (courage = red-orange, calm = blue-green, creativity = violet) through targeted chromatic bombardment.</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Moderate (30-50%)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Therapy (color-based emotional healing)</li>
            <li>Interrogation (inducing cooperation)</li>
            <li>Crowd control (mass calm/fear)</li>
            <li>Performance enhancement (courage/focus)</li>
            <li>Art (emotionally-responsive displays)</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Synergy with Luxomancy:</strong> Chromatic Manipulation provides precision targeting that Luxomancy's broad emotional influence lacks. Combined techniques extremely potent.</p>
          <p><strong>Limitation:</strong> Subject awareness reduces effect, prolonged exposure needed for deep states, cultural color associations vary (affects efficacy), some individuals color-blind or resistant.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="echo-swarm" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">19. Echo Swarm (Multi-Self-Illusion)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates hundreds of perfect visual copies of practitioner that move/act independently—ultimate confusion tactic. Each echo appears fully real (solid-seeming, casts shadows, makes sounds). Practitioner controls all echoes like puppet master.</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> High (45-65%, scales with echo count)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Combat (overwhelming opponents with numbers)</li>
            <li>Escape (enemies cannot identify real target)</li>
            <li>Reconnaissance (echoes scout while practitioner safe)</li>
            <li>Entertainment</li>
            <li>Psychological warfare</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Cognitive Load:</strong> Controlling 50+ echoes simultaneously requires extraordinary mental capacity—most practitioners limited to 20-30 echoes. Exceeding capacity causes echoes to behave erratically or reveals real practitioner.</p>
          <p><strong>Limitation:</strong> Echoes are illusions (intangible, pass through matter), skilled observers detect which is real (subtle tells like dust disturbance), extreme cognitive load, thread-expensive.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="reality-anchor" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">20. Reality Anchor (Illusion-Permanence)</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Makes illusions permanent—holograms that persist indefinitely without thread maintenance, becoming quasi-real through observer belief. Exploits quantum observer effect: sufficient observation collapses illusion into reality.</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Extreme initial (75-90%), zero ongoing (truly permanent)</p>
          <p><strong>Applications:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Permanent architecture (illusory buildings becoming real through collective belief)</li>
            <li>Artistic installations</li>
            <li>Memory preservation (anchoring visual memories as permanent holograms)</li>
            <li>Historical recreation</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Philosophical Question:</strong> When does illusion anchored by belief become "real"? If everyone believes it exists, observes it existing, interacts with it as real—is it real?</p>
          <p><strong>Limitation:</strong> Requires multiple observers (minimum 5-10) witnessing simultaneously, belief/observation must be genuine (knowing it's illusion prevents anchoring), some illusions resist permanence (complex or contradictory ones), creates entropy debt (permanence always expensive).</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div id="spectral-singularity" class="qw-card" style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
        <h3 class="qw-subtitle" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">21. Spectral Singularity (Light-Black-Hole) [Theoretical/Forbidden]</h3>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <p><strong>Function:</strong> Creates photon trap that absorbs all light—region of absolute darkness where photons cannot escape. Theoretical "optical event horizon" where electromagnetic radiation captured permanently.</p>
          <p><strong>Thread Cost:</strong> Extreme (85-95%)</p>
          <p><strong>Theoretical Results:</strong></p>
          <ul>
            <li>Perfect darkness (zero photons escape)</li>
            <li>Accumulating photonic energy inside (pressure builds)</li>
            <li>Eventual explosive release (all trapped light released at once as devastating EM burst)</li>
          </ul>
          <p><strong>Why Forbidden:</strong> Unstable, eventual collapse creates EM pulse destroying electronics/blinding people for kilometers, may trap non-photonic quanta (creating actual singularity), attempts catastrophic.</p>
          <p><strong>Limitation:</strong> Theoretical only, attempts fail violently, may be impossible with current QW understanding.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section id="risks" style="max-width: 900px; margin: 4rem 0;">
      <div class="qw-card">
        <h2 class="qw-title" style="color: var(--qw-spectra);">Risks & Failure States</h2>
        <div class="qw-body">
          <ul>
            <li><strong>Spectral Bleed:</strong> Illusions bleeding into reality or vice versa, causing perceptual confusion</li>
            <li><strong>Photon Overload:</strong> Too much light causing temporary or permanent blindness</li>
            <li><strong>Echo Persistence:</strong> Holographic constructs failing to dissipate, cluttering the Weave</li>
            <li><strong>Identity Blur:</strong> Practitioner losing track of real vs. illusory while deeply immersed</li>
            <li><strong>Chromatic Backlash:</strong> Uncontrolled color shifts affecting the Weaver's vision</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>
